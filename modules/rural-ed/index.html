Nurse-Led 2-Person Team ALS

Peri-Arrest to ROSC

A 68-year-old male arrives peri-arrest via volunteer ambulance with acute coronary syndrome. He deteriorates into ventricular fibrillation within minutes. RN and EN lead a 24-minute resuscitation in a 2-person team until the doctor arrives. Patient achieves ROSC and is retrieved by MedSTAR for definitive cardiac care.

Focus: Nurse-led ALS, fatigue management, medication safety, team communication, ROSC recognition
Duration: 76 minutes
Team: RN + EN (doctor arrives at 12‚Äì15 min mark)
Outcome: ROSC achieved at 24 minutes

Chest Pain to Sudden Arrest

A 65-year-old male presents with acute chest pain and is triaged as ATS Category 2. Initial assessment shows STEMI on ECG. Patient is stabilised and prepared for MedSTAR retrieval. Minutes before retrieval arrives, patient arrests into VF. Full ANZCOR ALS protocol is run with doctor present. Scenario explores rapid deterioration and resuscitation in a fully-staffed scenario.

Focus: ACS recognition, STEMI escalation, sudden arrest, full team resuscitation
Duration: 65 minutes
Team: RN + EN + Doctor (on-site)
Outcome: Variable (ROSC, ongoing arrest, or termination)

Rural ED Context

These scenarios are designed for small rural SA hospitals (Level 4‚Äì5 ED) with:

  • Limited staffing (often 2 RNs + 1 EN during off-hours)
  • Doctor on call, 10‚Äì15 minutes away
  • No CT, MRI, or on-site cardiologist
  • After-hours pathology limitations
  • MedSTAR retrieval 40‚Äì60 minutes away
  • Strong community connection (staff know patients)

The scenarios reflect the reality of rural emergency nursing: you are the first responder, you often lead initial care, your team is small but skilled, and the decisions you make in the first minutes can save lives.
